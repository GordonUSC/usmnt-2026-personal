The clothes that carry the story

USMNT Kits.

A team's kit is part of its mythology. USMNT has worn the entire range from beloved-cult-classic to widely-mocked-disaster. Sometimes those were the same kit. This is a tour through the ones that matter, and where the 2026 home shirt sits in the story.

The classics

Kits that deserve their own room.

The 1994 Denim

1994 home · adidas · Italia 90 cycle + WC 94

The kit that built the cult. Faux-denim front. Red and blue stars scattered across the chest. Worn at the home World Cup. Eric Wynalda, Tab Ramos, Alexi Lalas, Cobi Jones, Tony Meola. Universally regarded as one of the great World Cup kits ever made. Vintage versions sell for hundreds of dollars on eBay.

The 1994 Striped Away (Stars and Stripes)

1994 away · adidas

Red and white vertical stripes. Worn vs Switzerland in the group stage. The aesthetic counterpart to the denim. Together they are the visual identity of the 1994 World Cup for soccer-side memory.

The 2014 Bomb Pop

2014 away · Nike · World Cup Brazil

Horizontal red and blue popsicle stripes on white. Worn during the 2-2 vs Portugal that ended on a 95th-minute equalizer. Dempsey's nose was bloody. Bradley's hair was wet. The shirt was beautiful. Polarizing at release. Beloved now.

The 2010 White Sash Home

2010 home · Nike · World Cup South Africa

White with a single blue sash running from shoulder to waist. Clean, minimal, classic. Worn during the Donovan-vs-Algeria moment. Donovan in this kit, sliding to score, is one of the most-reproduced images in American soccer.

The 2018 Hoops

2018 cycle · Nike

USA's 2018 cycle had a horizontal-stripe shirt that lives in the supporter memory as the "hoops" kit. USA failed to qualify for the 2018 World Cup, so it never appeared at a major tournament. The kit and the Trinidad disaster are linked in fan memory forever.

The 2022 White Home

2022 home · Nike · World Cup Qatar

White base. Worn during Pulisic's Iran goal and the R16 run. The shirt of the modern-era return to the World Cup.

The current chapter

The 2026 kits.

2026 Home

Nike · home soil edition

The kit USMNT will wear at SoFi for the opener vs Paraguay. The first home shirt worn at a home World Cup since the 1994 denim. Whatever Nike's design tells, it's the one being photographed every game from June 12 to July 19.

The patches and crests

The badge.

1995: The blue-and-red shield with stars on white. The badge we wore through the 2002 Quarterfinal and the 2009 Spain win.

2016: The simplified red, white, and blue waved-flag crest. Still in use. The badge we wore through 2022 Qatar and the Pochettino era.

The patch: A small star above the crest counts each Gold Cup win. There are currently 7 stars above the badge.
