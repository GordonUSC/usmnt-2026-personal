Pochettino's Identity Project

How USA will play.

Vertical. Intense. Possession-with-purpose. Pochettino is building a team that wants the ball, presses high to get it back, and trusts its athletes in transition. He took the job in September 2024 and has been in identity-installation mode ever since.

The shape

4-2-3-1, with a 3-4-3 in the pocket.

Default: 4-2-3-1 (speculation)
The base formation

One striker (Balogun). Three behind: Pulisic on the left, Reyna or Tillman in the middle, Weah on the right. Two pivot midfielders: Adams paired with McKennie or Berhalter. Back four: Robinson, Ream, Richards, Scally (or Dest if healthy).

Why this shape: protects Pulisic's freedom, gives Adams cover, lets the fullbacks push high when the build-up is settled.

Note on accuracy: the specific starting eleven is speculation. Pochettino has not announced it. Adams as the 6 and Pulisic on the left are the only structural locks. Everything else could shift between the Senegal and Germany friendlies before the opener.

Plan B: 3-4-3
Game-state alternate

Three centerbacks (Ream, Richards, McKenzie). Wing-backs (Robinson, Weah) wider. Three forwards (Pulisic, Balogun, Reyna).

When Pochettino reaches for it: against a packed midfield like Paraguay's. Or when chasing late.

The spine

The three relationships that decide everything.

Adams + (McKennie or Berhalter)
The double pivot

With Yunus Musah controversially left off the final 26, the double pivot will pair Tyler Adams with either Weston McKennie (physicality, late runs) or Sebastian Berhalter (passing range, MLS form). Adams' health is the foundation of everything. The partner choice is Pochettino's biggest tactical question.

Pulisic + Balogun
The decisive connection

The 10-and-9 axis. Pulisic drifting in from the left, Balogun playing on the shoulder. Goals at this tournament will mostly come through this pair.

Robinson + Ream
The left side back

Jedi gives the team width going forward. Ream holds shape behind him. Without that balance, USA is leaking transitions on the left.

Where it could break

Honest assessment.

Pulisic's form

Seventeen league matches without a goal, a drought running since December 28. He comes in cold. If the opener goes poorly, the team's psychological center of gravity is in trouble.

Backline vs elite attacks

Belgium beat USA 5-2 in March. Portugal beat them 2-0 three days later. The Pochettino back four can be unsettled by combinations and pace. Against Brazil, Spain, or Türkiye, this is the question.

Set-piece defense

USA has been good attacking from set pieces (Pulisic delivery is elite). They have been less good defending them. Türkiye's Çalhanoğlu is a perfect storm of a corner taker.

Sub depth in attack

If Pulisic or Balogun cools off, the dropoff is real. Aaronson, Tillman, and Wright are useful pieces, but none is the same level of solo creator.

What we want to see

The signs USA is on.

1. Vertical entries through midfield. Without Musah on the roster, USA needs Adams + McKennie / Berhalter to do the line-breaking. If midfielders carry past the first line, things happen. If they don't, they don't.

2. Pulisic finishing chances. Not creating. Finishing. His actual goal-output is the leading indicator.

3. Adams covering the box. If he's clearing crosses and bumping attackers off the ball in his own 18, the rest of the back line stays compact.

4. Subs that change the game. Pochettino with a working bench is a contender. Pochettino with a one-eleven-deep group is a Round of 16 team.
